# Insights:

## Content Type Distribution:
Movies dominate the Netflix catalog.
- Movies: 6,131 (~70%)
- TV Shows: 2,676 (~30%)

This indicates that Netflix's library is primarily movie-focused with TV shows forming a smaller but significant portion.

---

## Top Content-Producing Countries:
The United States contributes the largest share of content by a wide margin.

Top Contributors:
1. United States: 2,818
2. India: 972
3. United Kingdom: 419

This suggests that Netflix content is heavily concentrated in a few key production regions with U.S. leading significantly.

---

## Content Growth Over Time:
Content additions increased rapidly from 2016 and peaked around 2019.
- 2016: 418 titles.
- 2019: 1,999 titles (peak).
- 2020 - 2021: slight decline.

This indicates a period of aggressive expansion followed by a slowdown in content additions.

---

## Genre Distribution:
The most common genres are:
- International Movies: 2,752
- Dramas: 2,427
- Comedies: 1,674

This suggests that Netflix places strong emphasis on international content and general audience genres such as drama and comedy.

---

## Content Trends by Type:
Movies consistently outnumber TV Shows across all years.

However, TV Shows show steady growth over time:
- TV content increases significantly after 2016.
- The gap between Movies and TV Shows narrows slightly in later years.

This suggests a gradual shift toward more serialized content, although Movies remain dominant.

---

## Key Takeaways:
- Netflix's catalog is heavily movie-driven.
- Content production is concentrated in a small number of countries.
- Rapid platform expansion occurred between 2016 and 2019.
- International content plays a major role in the catalog.
- TV Shows are growing but have not overtaken Movies.

---

## Limitations:
- Some records contain missing country and date information.
- Time-based analysis excludes entries with missing 'date_added'.
- Multi-value fields (e.g. genres, countries) were not fully normalized.

These limitations should be considered when interpreting the results.
